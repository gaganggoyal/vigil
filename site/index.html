Why it runs on a potato

It never re-encodes video. It just moves bytes.

Most recorders burn the CPU decoding and re-compressing every frame. Vigil copies the camera's stream straight to disk — -c copy. A 10-year-old dual-core barely notices, so it can quietly record several cameras at once.

# the whole trick, one flag:
ffmpeg -rtsp_transport tcp -i rtsp://cam \
  -c copy ← no decode, no re-encode \
  -f segment -segment_time 600 out_%T.mp4
Vigil · stream-copy~3% CPU
Typical re-encoding NVR~87% CPU

Illustrative, 4 cameras @ 1080p on an old dual-core. Recording stays near-idle; only live preview spends real CPU — and only while you're watching.

The magic trick

A disk that recycles itself.

This is how you stop buying storage. Vigil keeps recording and, when the drive nears full, it quietly drops the oldest clips to make room for new ones. A single cheap 2 TB USB drive can hold weeks — and it never overflows.

Good to know

Questions before you plug in.

Will my camera work?

Almost certainly. Nearly every WiFi/IP camera exposes an rtsp:// stream — TP-Link/Tapo, Hikvision, Dahua, Amcrest, Reolink, and generic ONVIF cams all do. If it has an RTSP address, Vigil can record it. Use Scan to auto-find them.

How old a computer can I use?

Older than you'd think. Because recording is stream-copied (no re-encoding), a decade-old dual-core with 4 GB RAM can record several cameras. Any machine that runs Node 18+ and ffmpeg will do — Windows, macOS, Linux, even a Raspberry Pi.

Do I need internet or an account?

No. Everything runs on your local network. No sign-up, no cloud upload, no monthly fee. For remote viewing, use a home VPN rather than exposing it to the internet.

Where does the video go?

To whatever folder you choose — your internal drive or, better, a cheap external USB drive. Clips are plain MP4 files organized by camera and time, so you can copy or back them up like any other file.

What does it cost?

Nothing. Vigil is free and open source under the MIT license. Your only cost is a disk you probably already own.
